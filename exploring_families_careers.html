Sentence Patterns
Three key patterns to master!
PATTERN 1 — IDENTIFYING PEOPLE
Who's he? / Who's she?
He's my father. He's a doctor.
👉 "Who's she? She's my aunt. She's a teacher."
PATTERN 2 — ASKING ABOUT OCCUPATIONS
What does your mother do?
She's a nurse.
👉 "What does your father do? He's a police officer."
PATTERN 3 — INTRODUCTION
This is my father. He is a teacher.
👉 "This is my sister. She is a student."

I am + Verb-ing
Describing what we are doing RIGHT NOW!
🧠Grammar Secret: Use "I'm + V-ing" to talk about what you are doing right now!
📖 READING
"I'm reading a story about a firefighter."
🏗️ BUILDING
"I'm building a big tower with blocks."
🎵 SINGING
"I'm singing a happy song for my family."
🤝 HELPING
"I'm helping my teacher in the classroom."
👩‍🏫 TEACHING
"I'm teaching students English."
🚌 DRIVING
"I'm driving a big red bus."

Using A / AN with Jobs
✅ Use "A" (consonant sound)
a teacher | a doctor | a farmer | a nurse | a baker | a cook | a pilot
✅ Use "AN" (vowel sound)
an engineer | an artist | an actor | an active girl
