Active Voice & Passive Voice

Who does the action? Learn the difference — and have fun doing it! 🎉

1. What Is "Voice" in Grammar?
Understanding the big idea

In English, "voice" tells us who is doing the action in a sentence. There are two voices:

🟠 Active Voice
The Doer comes FIRST
The subject of the sentence does the action. It is direct, clear, and strong.
Subject → does → Object
"The cat chased the mouse."
🔵 Passive Voice
The Receiver comes FIRST
The object of the action becomes the subject. The doer is often hidden or comes at the end with "by".
Object → is/was/will be → done (by Subject)
"The mouse was chased by the cat."
💡 Simple Rule: In Active Voice → the subject does something. In Passive Voice → something is done to the subject!
🟠
2. Active Voice — Explained
Clear, direct, and powerful!
📌 Formula:  Subject + Verb + Object
Active 🐶 The dog eats the bone.
Active 👩 Sara wrote a letter.
Active 🧒 The boy kicked the ball.
Active 👩‍🍳 Mum baked a chocolate cake.
Active 🏫 The teacher will explain the lesson.

✅ When to use Active Voice:

  • 🟠 When you want to say clearly who did the action
  • 🟠 When writing stories, emails, or conversations
  • 🟠 When you want short, punchy sentences
🔵
3. Passive Voice — Explained
When the action is more important than who did it
📌 Formula:  Object + is/was/will be + Past Participle (+ by Subject)
Passive 🦴 The bone is eaten by the dog.
Passive 📄 A letter was written by Sara.
Passive ⚽ The ball was kicked by the boy.
Passive 🎂 A chocolate cake was baked by Mum.
Passive 📚 The lesson will be explained by the teacher.

✅ When to use Passive Voice:

  • 🔵 When the doer is unknown — "The window was broken."
  • 🔵 When the action is more important than who did it
  • 🔵 In reports, science writing, and news
🔄
4. Active ↔ Passive Conversions
See how sentences flip!
Present Tense
🟠 The chef cooks the food.
⇄
🔵 The food is cooked by the chef.
Past Tense
🟠 She read the book.
⇄
🔵 The book was read by her.
Future Tense
🟠 They will build a house.
⇄
🔵 A house will be built by them.
Present — Animals 🐾
🟠 The lion hunts the deer.
⇄
🔵 The deer is hunted by the lion.
Past — At School 🏫
🟠 The teacher marked the papers.
⇄
🔵 The papers were marked by the teacher.

5. Tenses + Voice — Big Table
Present, Past & Future in both voices
Tense 🟠 Active Voice 🔵 Passive Voice 🔊
🟢 Present She writes a poem. A poem is written by her.
🔵 Past He ate the apple. The apple was eaten by him.
🟡 Future We will plant trees. Trees will be planted by us.
🟢 Present Tom fixes the bike. The bike is fixed by Tom.
🔵 Past The wind broke the window. The window was broken by the wind.
🟡 Future Dad will cook dinner. Dinner will be cooked by Dad.

10. Closing — Remember This!
The golden summary
🟠
Active Voice
Subject → does → the action
The cat chased the mouse.
Direct · Clear · Strong
🔵
Passive Voice
Object → receives → the action
The mouse was chased by the cat.
Used when doer is unknown

🌈 Simple Rules for Kids:

  • 🟠 Active = Subject DOES something
  • 🔵 Passive = Something IS DONE to the subject
  • 🟢 Present = Now  |  verb form: is/are + past participle
  • 🔵 Past = Before  |  verb form: was/were + past participle
  • 🟡 Future = Later  |  verb form: will be + past participle
