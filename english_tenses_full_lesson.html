1. Learning Objectives
What students will learn
  • ⏰Understand time — present, past, and future
  • 🔍Recognise different tense forms
  • ✍️Use simple phrases and sentences in each tense
🧠
2. Warm-Up (5 min)
Get students thinking about time

Teacher writes these on the board — ask: When does each happen?

I eat.
I ate.
I will eat.
👉 Tense = Time
🟢
Section 1: Present Tense
Actions happening now or regularly
🟢
1.1 Present Simple
Habits and daily actions
👉 Habit / daily action
Example words:
🔊 eat 🔊 play 🔊 go 🔊 read
Signal words:
every dayalwaysoften
Sentences:
  • I play football.
  • She eats rice.
  • We go to school every day.
🟢
1.2 Present Continuous
Happening right now
Form: am / is / are + verb-ing
🔊 playing 🔊 eating 🔊 reading
  • I am eating.
  • She is playing.
  • They are reading.
🟢
1.3 Present Perfect
Happened before, still important now
Form: have / has + past participle
🔊 eaten 🔊 gone 🔊 seen
  • I have eaten lunch.
  • She has finished her homework.
🟢
1.4 Present Perfect Continuous
Started before, still continuing
Form: have / has been + verb-ing
  • I have been studying.
  • She has been playing for an hour.
🔵
Section 2: Past Tense
Actions that already happened
🔵
2.1 Past Simple
Finished action in the past
👉 Finished action
🔊 ate 🔊 played 🔊 went
yesterdaylast night
  • I played soccer yesterday.
  • She went home.
🔵
2.2 Past Continuous
Action in progress in the past
Form: was / were + verb-ing
  • I was eating.
  • They were playing.
🔵
2.3 Past Perfect
Happened before another past action
Form: had + past participle
  • I had eaten before school.
  • She had finished her work.
🔵
2.4 Past Perfect Continuous
Action continued before another past time
Form: had been + verb-ing
  • I had been studying for two hours.
  • She had been playing before dinner.
🟡
Section 3: Future Tense
Actions that will happen later
🟡
3.1 Future Simple
Action that will happen
Form: will + verb
🔊 will eat 🔊 will go
tomorrownext weeksoonlater
  • I will eat dinner.
  • She will go to school.
🟡
3.2 Future Continuous
Will be happening in the future
Form: will be + verb-ing
  • I will be studying.
  • They will be playing.
🟡
3.3 Future Perfect
Will be finished before a future time
Form: will have + past participle
  • I will have finished homework.
  • She will have eaten by 8 PM.
🟡
3.4 Future Perfect Continuous
Will continue until a future time
Form: will have been + verb-ing
  • I will have been studying for two hours.
  • She will have been playing all day.
