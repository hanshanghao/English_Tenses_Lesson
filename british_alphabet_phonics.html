Vowel (5)

A vowel is an open sound — air flows freely through the mouth with no blocking. The 5 vowels are A, E, I, O, U. Every English word contains at least one vowel.

Consonant (21)

A consonant is a shaped sound — the lips, tongue, or teeth block or redirect the air. All 21 remaining letters are consonants: B, C, D, F, G … X, Y, Z.

ⓘ How to Use This Tool

Here's your interactive British Alphabet Phonics tool! Here's how to use it:

▶ Tap any letter card to hear it spoken aloud in British RP (Received Pronunciation) using your device's speech synthesis engine. Each card shows the uppercase and lowercase letter, its IPA phonetic symbol, and whether it's a vowel or consonant.

◆ Filter buttons let you focus on just Vowels (A, E, I, O, U) or all 21 Consonants.

▶ Play All runs through the alphabet in sequence — great for singing / reciting practice.

🇬🇧 A few British-specific pronunciations to note:

  • H is "aitch" (not "haitch") — the H in the letter name is silent in British RP
  • Z is "zed" (not "zee") — always "zed" in British English
  • R is non-rhotic — /ɑː/ — softer and not rolled; R after a vowel is not pronounced
  • W is the full "double-you" — /ˈdʌbəljuː/ — all four syllables spoken clearly
