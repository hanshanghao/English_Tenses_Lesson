What is Eid ul Fitr?

Eid ul Fitr (عيد الفطر) — "Festival of Breaking the Fast" — is one of the two major Islamic holidays. It falls on 1 Shawwal, the tenth month of the Islamic calendar, immediately after Ramadan ends. In 2026, Eid ul Fitr is expected on 19–20 March 2026 in Taiwan.

قَدْ أَفْلَحَ مَن تَزَكَّىٰ وَذَكَرَ اسْمَ رَبِّهِ فَصَلَّىٰ
"He has certainly succeeded who purifies himself. And mentions the name of his Lord and prays."
— Surah Al-A'la 87:14–15

🌙 Origin & Meaning

Eid ul Fitr was established in the 2nd year of Hijri (624 CE) in Madinah when the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ observed that people were celebrating two days from their pre-Islamic traditions. He declared that Allah had replaced those days with two better celebrations: Eid ul Fitr and Eid ul Adha.

The word "Eid" means "festival" or "returning happiness" in Arabic. "Fitr" means "breaking the fast." Together, Eid ul Fitr celebrates the successful completion of Ramadan — the spiritual journey of a month — and the gift of Allah's mercy and forgiveness.

📅 2026 Date for Taiwan

Eid ul Fitr 2026 is expected on 19 or 20 March 2026 (1 Shawwal 1447H), depending on the official moon sighting announcement. Celebrations typically last 3 days.

✨ Key Practices of Eid ul Fitr

🌙
Moon Sighting

Eid begins with the official sighting of the crescent moon of Shawwal. When confirmed, Muslims announce "Eid Mubarak!" and the celebrations officially begin at sunset.

🛁
Ghusl & Dress Up

On Eid morning, Muslims perform a full ritual bath (Ghusl), wear their best or new clothes, apply perfume (Oud or Attar), and have a small sweet breakfast before the Eid prayer.

🤲
Zakat al-Fitr

Every Muslim must pay Zakat al-Fitr before the Eid prayer — a compulsory charity of food or its monetary equivalent to help the poor celebrate Eid. It must be paid before the Eid prayer begins.

🎁
Gifts (Eidiyah)

Children receive monetary gifts (Eidiyah) from parents, grandparents, and relatives. Adults exchange sweets, desserts, and visits. "Eid Mubarak!" and "Taqabbal Allahu Minna Wa Minkum" are common greetings.

🍽️
Feasting Together

After the Eid prayer, families gather for elaborate feasts. Traditional Eid foods include Sheer Khurma (vermicelli pudding), biryani, kebabs, baklava, and various regional sweets and desserts.

🏡
Visiting Family

Muslims visit relatives, friends, and neighbours. Cemeteries are also visited to pray for deceased family members. Acts of charity and forgiveness are strongly encouraged on Eid day.

The Eid Prayer & Rituals

The Eid ul Fitr prayer (Salat al-Eid) is one of the most powerful and communal acts in Islam. Thousands of Muslims gather in mosques, open fields, and stadiums to pray together in congregation, marking the joyous occasion with "Allahu Akbar!" (Takbir) ringing out across the grounds.

🕌 Salat al-Eid (Eid Prayer)

The Eid prayer consists of two Rak'ahs (units of prayer) with additional Takbeers (saying "Allahu Akbar"). It is prayed in congregation, usually in the morning between sunrise and Dhuhr time.

The Imam delivers a Khutbah (sermon) after the prayer, covering topics of gratitude, charity, unity, and Islamic guidance for the new month ahead. The prayer is attended by men, women, and children — even menstruating women who don't pray still attend for the gathering and sermon.

📍 Eid Prayer Locations in Taiwan

Major Eid prayers in Taiwan are held at Taipei Grand Mosque, Taichung Mosque, Tainan Mosque, Longgang Mosque (Zhongli), and Kaohsiung Islamic Centre. Check with local mosques for 2026 times.

🌅 Step-by-Step Eid Morning Guide

Wake Up Early

Rise before or at Fajr time. Make intention (Niyyah) for the blessed day of Eid.

Ghusl (Bath)

Perform a full ritual bath — Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ for Eid day.

Dress in Best Clothes

Wear new or best garments. Apply Oud or Attar (natural perfume). Smile!

Eat Before Prayer

Unlike Eid ul Adha, on Eid ul Fitr it is Sunnah to eat something sweet (dates, dessert) BEFORE the prayer.

Pay Zakat al-Fitr

Must be paid before the Eid prayer. Help the poor celebrate Eid too.

Go to Mosque Early

Walk (Sunnah) to the mosque reciting Takbeer: "Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah..."

Perform Eid Prayer

Pray 2 Rak'ahs with extra Takbeers. Listen to the Khutbah (sermon) attentively.

Greet & Celebrate!

Hug, shake hands, say "Eid Mubarak!" Visit family, share food, give gifts.

Eid ul Fitr in Taiwan

Taiwan is home to a growing and vibrant Muslim community. From the historic Taipei Grand Mosque to newer Islamic centres in Taichung and Kaohsiung, Muslims across the island celebrate Eid ul Fitr with warmth, unity, and joyous festivities.

🇹🇼 Muslims in Taiwan

Taiwan's Muslim population includes local Taiwanese Muslims, foreign workers (mainly from Indonesia, Malaysia, and South Asia), and students. The community is served by several mosques and Islamic organisations.

The Taipei Grand Mosque (臺北清真寺), built in 1960, is the most prominent mosque in Taiwan and a hub for the Eid ul Fitr celebrations. It holds large congregation prayers attended by thousands.

🕌 Major Mosques in Taiwan

Taipei Grand Mosque · Taichung Mosque · Tainan Mosque · Longgang Mosque (Zhongli) · Kaohsiung Islamic Center · Da'an Mosque (Taipei)

🤝
Community Unity

On Eid morning, Muslims from all backgrounds — Indonesian workers, Malaysian students, South Asian residents, and Taiwanese converts — pray shoulder to shoulder at Taiwan's mosques, embodying Islamic brotherhood.

🍛
Multicultural Eid Feasts

Eid feasts in Taiwan reflect the community's diversity: Indonesian rendang, Malaysian laksa, Pakistani biryani, Arab lamb dishes, and Taiwanese halal fusion foods all share the same table.

🏙️
Halal Food Scene

Taiwan has a growing halal food scene with certified restaurants in Taipei's Da'an district, Zhongli (home to a large Indonesian community), and Taichung. Many restaurants offer special Eid menus.

📱
Community Apps & Groups

Taiwan's Muslim community stays connected via WhatsApp, LINE groups, and the Taiwan Muslim Brotherhood (中華民國回教協會) website for Eid prayer times, moon sighting announcements, and community events.

Morning of 19 March 2026: Eid Prayer at mosques across Taiwan (approx. 7:00–8:30 AM)
19–21 March 2026: Three days of Eid celebration — family visits, feasts, and community gatherings
