About the Occasions

Three Sacred Occasions of Islam

Islam has two official festivals — Eid ul Fitr and Eid ul Adha — preceded by Ramadan, the holiest month of the Islamic calendar. Together, these occasions shape the spiritual and social life of Taiwan's Muslim community of approximately 60,000 to 200,000 Muslims.

→
🌙 Holy Month
Ramadan 2026

The ninth and holiest month of the Islamic calendar. 30 days of dawn-to-dusk fasting, prayer, Quran recitation, and spiritual reflection.

📅18 Feb – 18 Mar 2026 · 1 Ramadan 1447H
→
🎉 Festival of Breaking Fast
Eid ul Fitr 2026

The joyous celebration marking the end of Ramadan. Special Eid prayers, feasts, gifts for children, and community gatherings across Taiwan.

📅19–20 March 2026 · 1 Shawwal 1447H
→
🐑 Festival of Sacrifice
Eid ul Adha 2026

The greater of the two Eids. Commemorates Prophet Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son. Observed with Qurbani, Hajj pilgrimage, and prayers.

📅27 May 2026 · 10 Dhul Hijjah 1447H

Quick Reference

Key Dates & Facts

🌙
Ramadan Begins
18 February 2026 (1 Ramadan 1447H). Subject to moon sighting. Taiwan fajr in Taipei ~06:10 AM.
🌅
Eid ul Fitr
19–20 March 2026 (1 Shawwal 1447H). Celebrated at the end of Ramadan's 30 days of fasting.
🐑
Eid ul Adha
27 May 2026 (10 Dhul Hijjah 1447H). The greater Eid, coinciding with the completion of Hajj in Mecca.
📿
Islamic Calendar
The Hijri calendar is lunar-based, 354 days/year. 2026 spans 1447–1448 AH. New year 1448H: 16 June 2026.
🕌
Muslims in Taiwan
~60,000–200,000 Muslims in Taiwan. Major mosques in Taipei, Taichung, Tainan, Kaohsiung, and Longgang.
🌍
Night of Power
Laylat al-Qadr falls on the 27th night of Ramadan — expected 16 March 2026. The holiest night of the year.
