The Story of Ibrahim & Ismail

Eid ul Adha (عيد الأضحى) — the "Festival of Sacrifice" — commemorates one of the most powerful stories in all of Islamic tradition: Prophet Ibrahim's (Abraham's) willingness to sacrifice his beloved son Ismail in complete obedience to Allah's command.

فَلَمَّا أَسْلَمَا وَتَلَّهُ لِلْجَبِينِ ۚ وَنَادَيْنَاهُ أَن يَا إِبْرَاهِيمُ ۝ قَدْ صَدَّقْتَ الرُّؤْيَا
"And when they had both submitted and he put him down upon his forehead, We called to him: O Ibrahim! You have fulfilled the vision."
— Surah As-Saffat 37:103–104

📖 The Story of the Great Sacrifice

Prophet Ibrahim (AS) had longed for a child for many years. Allah blessed him with his son Ismail (AS). Then, Allah tested Ibrahim with the ultimate test: a vision commanding him to sacrifice his beloved son.

Ibrahim told Ismail of the vision. Ismail, reflecting his father's faith, replied: "Father, do what you are commanded. You will find me patient, if Allah wills." As Ibrahim prepared to carry out Allah's command, Allah intervened and replaced Ismail with a ram to be sacrificed instead — as a reward for Ibrahim's absolute submission and trust in Allah.

This act of complete faith, surrender, and obedience is commemorated every year on 10 Dhul Hijjah — Eid ul Adha. Muslims worldwide sacrifice an animal (Qurbani) in remembrance of this supreme act of devotion.

📅
2026 Date

Eid ul Adha 2026 is expected on 27 May 2026 (10 Dhul Hijjah 1447H). Celebrations continue for 3 days (Ayyam al-Tashreeq) until 12 Dhul Hijjah.

🌍
Global Significance

Eid ul Adha is considered the greater of the two Islamic Eids. It coincides with the completion of Hajj — the annual pilgrimage to Mecca performed by millions of Muslims worldwide.

🤝
Message of Faith

The core message of Eid ul Adha is complete submission (Islam) to Allah. It teaches sacrifice, generosity, gratitude, and sharing with the poor — values central to Islamic life.

Qurbani, Prayer & Celebration

The three pillars of Eid ul Adha are: the Eid Prayer, the Qurbani (animal sacrifice), and the sharing of meat with family, neighbours, and the poor. Together, they represent worship, sacrifice, and community solidarity.

🕌 The Eid ul Adha Prayer

Like Eid ul Fitr, the day begins with the congregation Eid prayer (Salat al-Eid) — two Rak'ahs with extra Takbeers, followed by a Khutbah (sermon). The key difference: on Eid ul Adha, it is Sunnah to NOT eat before the prayer, waiting to eat from the Qurbani meat after the prayer.

Takbeer of Tashreeq is recited after every obligatory prayer from Fajr of 9 Dhul Hijjah (26 May 2026) until Asr of 13 Dhul Hijjah (30 May 2026).

Who Must Do Qurbani?

Qurbani is obligatory (Wajib) for every adult Muslim who possesses the Nisab (wealth threshold ~3 oz of gold). It must be performed after the Eid prayer on 10, 11, or 12 Dhul Hijjah.

🍖
Distributing the Meat

The meat is divided into 3 equal parts: ⅓ for the family, ⅓ for relatives and friends, ⅓ for the poor and needy. This distribution embodies Islamic values of generosity and social equity.

🇹🇼
Qurbani in Taiwan

In Taiwan, Muslims can arrange Qurbani through local mosques and halal butchers, or donate to Islamic charities that perform Qurbani on their behalf in countries where meat is most needed.

Hajj & The Days of Dhul Hijjah

Eid ul Adha coincides with the completion of Hajj — the fifth pillar of Islam and the greatest annual gathering of humanity. Millions of Muslims from every corner of the world converge on Mecca during the first 10 days of Dhul Hijjah, culminating in Eid ul Adha on 10 Dhul Hijjah.

وَأَذِّن فِي النَّاسِ بِالْحَجِّ يَأْتُوكَ رِجَالًا وَعَلَىٰ كُلِّ ضَامِرٍ
"And proclaim to the people the Hajj [pilgrimage]; they will come to you on foot and on every lean camel..."
— Surah Al-Hajj 22:27

🕋 What is Hajj?

Hajj is the annual pilgrimage to Mecca, Saudi Arabia — obligatory once in a lifetime for every Muslim who is physically and financially able. In 2026, Hajj takes place during Dhul Hijjah 1447H, with the peak on Arafah Day (9 Dhul Hijjah = 26 May 2026).

Hajj brings together over 2 million Muslims from 180+ countries in one of the most powerful displays of Islamic unity and equality — everyone wearing identical simple white Ihram garments, regardless of wealth or status.

🗓️ Hajj 2026 Key Dates

8 Dhul Hijjah (25 May): Yawm al-Tarwiyah
9 Dhul Hijjah (26 May): Day of Arafah — the climax of Hajj
10 Dhul Hijjah (27 May): Eid ul Adha — Qurbani Day
11–13 Dhul Hijjah (28–30 May): Ayyam al-Tashreeq

🕋 Key Rituals of Hajj

🤍

Ihram

Pilgrim enters sacred state wearing white cloth. All worldly distinctions removed.

🕌

Tawaf

7 counter-clockwise circuits around the Kaaba at Masjid al-Haram in Mecca.

🏃

Sa'i

Walking 7 times between Safa & Marwa hills, commemorating Hajar's search for water.

⛺

Mina

Staying overnight in the tent city of Mina (8 Dhul Hijjah) in preparation for Arafah.

🌄

Arafah

Standing on the plain of Arafah (9 Dhul Hijjah) — the climax of Hajj and greatest day.

🌙

Muzdalifah

Night under the stars collecting pebbles for the symbolic stoning of Shaytan.

🪨

Rami (Stoning)

Throwing pebbles at the Jamarat pillars symbolising rejection of evil (Shaytan).

✂️

Halq / Taqsir

Shaving or cutting the hair. Exiting the state of Ihram. Back to Mecca for Tawaf.

⭐ Day of Arafah — 26 May 2026 — For Non-Hajj Muslims

Even for Muslims who are not performing Hajj, the Day of Arafah (9 Dhul Hijjah) is the most virtuous day of the year. Fasting on this day is Sunnah and expiates sins of the previous AND coming year. "There is no day on which Allah frees more people from the Fire than the Day of Arafah." — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (Muslim)
